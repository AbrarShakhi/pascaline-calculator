Here’s a list of common scientific **operators / functions** you typically find on a scientific calculator. These may vary by model, but most scientific calculators include many of these:

---

## **Basic Trigonometric Functions**

* **sin** — sine
* **cos** — cosine
* **tan** — tangent
* **sin⁻¹** (arcsin)
* **cos⁻¹** (arccos)
* **tan⁻¹** (arctan)

## **Hyperbolic Trigonometric Functions**

* **sinh**
* **cosh**
* **tanh**
* **sinh⁻¹**, **cosh⁻¹**, **tanh⁻¹**

---

## **Logarithmic & Exponential**

* **log** (base 10)
* **ln** (natural log)
* **10ˣ**
* **eˣ**
* **xʸ** (power function)
* **√x**, **³√x**, **x^(1/n)**

---

## **Algebraic / Arithmetic**

* **+**, **−**, **×**, **÷**
* **x²**, **x³**
* **1/x**
* **%** (percentage)
* **|x|** (absolute value)
* **round**, **floor**, **ceil** (in some models)

---

## **Angle & Conversion**

* **DEG ↔ RAD ↔ GRAD**
* **° ' ''** (degrees–minutes–seconds)
* **→DMS**, **→DEG**

---

## **Statistical & Probability**

* **n!** (factorial)
* **nPr** (permutations)
* **nCr** (combinations)
* **Σx**, **Σx²**
* **standard deviation** functions (σₙ, σₙ₋₁)

---

## **Boolean / Logic (advanced models)**

* **AND**, **OR**, **XOR**, **NOT**
* **bit shift (<<, >>)**
* **binary/octal/hex modes**

---

## **Miscellaneous**

* **π**
* **Ans** (previous result)
* **EXP** (scientific notation)
* **ENG** (engineering notation)
* **M+**, **M−**, **MR**, **MC** (memory functions)
